Publications

  • On the link between Binomial Theorem and Discrete Convolution of Power Function


    In this manuscript we establish a relation between Binomial theorem and discrete convolution of power function. We show that Binomial expansion of \(s\)-powered binomial \((x+y)^s, \ s\geq1, \ x+y\geq 1\), \(x,y,s\in\mathbb{N}\) is equivalent to the sum of the products of discrete convolved power function and certain real coefficient. In addition, we generalise the relation between Binomial theorem and discrete convolution of power function to Multinomial case.

    • On the quantum differentiation of smooth real-valued functions
      Calculating the value of \(C^{k\in\{1,\infty\}}\) class of smoothness real-valued function's derivative in point of \(\mathbb{R}^+\) in radius of convergence of its aylor polynomial (or series), applying an analog of Newton's binomial theorem and \(q\)-difference operator. \((P,q)\)-power difference introduced in section 5. Additionally, by means of Newton's interpolation formula, the discrete analog of Taylor series interpolation using \(q\)-difference and \(p,q\)-power difference is shown.
